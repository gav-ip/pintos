### ALGORITHMS

> A2: Briefly describe what happens in a call to timer_sleep(), including the effects of the timer interrupt handler.

The caller sets its wakeup_tick, inserts itself into an ordered sleep list, and blocks. Each timer interrupt unblocks all threads whose wakeup_tick has been reached.

> A3: What steps are taken to minimize the amount of time spent in the timer interrupt handler?

To minimize the amount of time spent in the timer interrupt handler we have a wakeup_tick comparator to insert threads to a sleep lists which is sorted with the lowest sleep time

### SYNCHRONIZATION

> A4: How are race conditions avoided when multiple threads call timer_sleep() simultaneously?

Race conditions are avoided by disabling interrupts as a mutex. With interrupts off, the timer interrupt cannot run and no other thread can be scheduled.

> A5: How are race conditions avoided when a timer interrupt occurs during a call to timer_sleep()?

The critical section is protected by disabling interrupts before touching shared state. A timer interrupt cannot run in the middle of that section, so there is no race between timer_sleep() and the timer interrupt handler.

### RATIONALE

> A6: Why did you choose this design? In what ways is it superior to another design you considered?

Choosing this design for interrupt safety was important because timer_sleep is called with interrupts on. So there needs to be some form of synchornization that works in interrupt context. Else there will be some form of resource bottleneck and unnecessary use of complex data structures

#### RATIONALE

> B7: Why did you choose this design? In what ways is it superior to another design you considered?

Using a separate donor_elem lets a blocked thread be on both the semaphore waiters list and the holder’s donors list without corrupting either. Removing donors only for the released lock (donor-&gt;waiting_on_lock == lock) keeps priorities correct when a thread holds multiple locks.

#### ALGORITHMS

> C2: How is the way you divided the cost of scheduling between code inside and outside interrupt context likely to affect performance?

Keeping the timer interrupt short (only tick updates and waking sleepers) avoids blocking other interrupts and reduces jitter. Heavier work (donation, scheduling) in thread context lets the system remain responsive.

#### RATIONALE

> C3: Briefly critique your design, pointing out advantages and disadvantages in your design choices. If you were to have extra time to work on this part of the project, how might you choose to refine or improve your design?

Simple per-lock donor removal and chain walk work well. Downsides are O(n) list_max in sema_up, missing intr_disable in thread_set_priority, and a fixed depth limit of 8. Improvements: add intr_disable and consider a heap for donors.
